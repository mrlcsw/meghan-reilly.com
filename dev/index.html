Meghan Reilly

Therapist specializing in trauma

Welcome

Therapy is a brave and vulnerable undertaking that I hold with the utmost respect as you come to me with your needs and longings.

I am motivated by the belief that each person, given the right support, has the capacity to heal. I am humbled by the healing transformations I routinely witness and experience in my career.

I will partner with you to create a safe space in which we can together understand and explore the dynamics at play in your life. My work is grounded in both an understanding of the the neurobiology of attachment and that lasting change that can happen in compassionate connection with a safe other.

I will support you in working through your experience in a manageable way, so that you can track and experience the shifts you are seeking. I believe that the goal of therapy is not just to reduce symptoms but to live a life of greater enlivenment in the present.

Experience

I have a background in individual outpatient treatment, couples and family work, and treatment within the medical setting, including working with parents who have adopted children, and with parents during their children’s hospitalizations in a neonatal intensive care unit (NICU).

I draw from over 15 years of clinical practice in my treatment approach, and work from an integrative model of both relational approaches and body-centered interventions, to provide trauma-informed care.

I work with clients who struggle with anxiety, racing thoughts, depressed mood, conflict in relationships, chronic pain, difficulty regulating in relationship, trouble with intimacy, parenting concerns, ambivalence about certain aspects of their lives and more. I specialize in the treatment of complex trauma and dissociative disorders.

I enjoy working with both individuals and couples.

Trauma-Informed Care

Traumatic events of the past often get stuck in "trauma time" in the body and mind, and folks end up feeling like they are re-experienceing what happened then, as if it's happening now. The body has ingenious ways of adapting to overwhelming experience in an attempt to survive; the trouble is that in the present, these very strategies that were so helpful back then, often have costs that are too big now.

Trauma treatment is grounded in working in the present, in helping folks reconnect to their inner experience in safe ways, and in building a life that has flexibility and room for connection, creativity, and even joy. Trauma treatment will work to help bring folks from functioning in survival mode and into a life where they can thrive.

Values-Based Care
